RESEARCH QUALITATIVE

Exploring gen Z Slang Potential Effects Across Generation (Generation X, Millennials, and Generation Z)

ABSTRACT:

This study focused on the rising use of slang words among Generation Z and its effects on communication, language use, and reading, writing, and comprehension skills across different generations, including Generation X, Millennials, and Generation Z. The analysis aimed to identify how slang influences language dynamics and generational interactions. The results revealed that Generation Z heavily relies on technology and social media, making slang a common part of their daily communication. In contrast, Millennials, who prioritize work-life balance and value experiences,

use slang more moderately. Meanwhile, Generation X, known for preferring direct communication and stability, uses slang less frequently. The study also highlighted how these generational traits shape their language preferences and behaviors over time. The growing influence of slang, particularly among Generation Z, underscores its impact on language comprehension and communication dynamics. The findings emphasize the importance of understanding generational differences in language use to promote effective communication, education, and social interactions. The research provides valuable insights for educators, organizations, and individuals in addressing language evolution and bridging generational communication gaps.

Authors: Roilo B. Bala, James Yzekiel J. Balondo, Prince Reynald S. Paje, Micaella Audrey F. Pagar, Hannah Vanessa M. Quimora
